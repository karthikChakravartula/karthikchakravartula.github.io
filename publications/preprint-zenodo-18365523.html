Real-Time Credit Risk Assessment in Agri-Finance: A Dynamics 365 Based AI Framework

Author: Karthik Nallani Chakravartula

Status: Accepted / In progress publishing (preprint)

PDF: Download
Zenodo record: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18365473

Abstract

The agricultural finance industry is experiencing difficulties in assessing and managing credit risk as rapidly changing commodity prices, climate variability, and conventional assessment processes neglect the dynamic nature of the agricultural sector. This paper outlines a real-time credit risk assessment framework using Microsoft Dynamics 365’s AI capabilities for agricultural finance institutions. The framework targets the insufficiency of the 5 C’s of credit in assessing creditworthiness in agricultural financing (Character, Capacity, Capital, Collateral, and Conditions). The proposed system offers a unique combination of Microsoft Dynamics 365 Finance, AI Builder, Power BI, and Copilot for Finance, and it is fundamentally a vendor-agnostic enterprise architecture that augments existing banking infrastructure by avoiding complete system replacement. In contrast to the current fintech platforms which provide stand-alone credit scoring models, our framework looks to address the full complexity of rural agricultural risk mitigation. Our framework provides a Dynamic Risk Assessment Engine which creates a structured change to each of the traditional 5 C’s of credit applying a comprehensive real-time integration of weather APIs, satellite image, IoT sensors, commodity market feeds, and government agricultural databases. Using the framework, we show positive contributions in accuracy and predictive credit assessment examine default risk in loan applications. This full integration approach fills a critical gap between existing agricultural fintech services which address one data source or disrupt critical infrastructure. Decision error risks dropped from 23% more credit decision error than conventional methodology and our loan decision process dropped from 5–10 days to 3 days. The framework responds to core issues raised in the report by the FCA and FCSIC regarding concentrated risk in agricultural lending and the limited risk management capabilities of agricultural lenders. The framework seems useful as it was tested with 398 applications for microfinance based on the study of good practice in microfinance institutions. It provides evidence of potential changes to agricultural credit risk assessment practices.
