Reducing Cloud Storage Costs in Agri-Lending CRM Systems Using Intelligent Data Retention Policies

Authors: Karthik Nallani Chakravartula; Aravind Raghu

ORCID (Karthik): 0009-0007-0255-6060
ORCID (Aravind): 0009-0006-4340-3653

Status: Accepted / In progress publishing (preprint)

PDF: Download
Zenodo record: https://zenodo.org/records/18365525

Abstract

Agri-lending Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems store large collections of very sensitive data about farmers such as personal identification details, credit ratings, loan history, ownership papers, and creditworthiness. The development of historical records is essential to support informed lending, maintain compliance with regulatory tasks and processes, and to mitigate risk. There are valid reasons for us to keep the data for long periods of time; however, if agri-lending CRM is to maintain and retain full audit logs and history records, it will result in extremely high operational costs and poor system performance. In this paper we want to present an intelligent data retention practice to deal with these challenges, by furnishing a simple 30-day retention period, and a process to archive historical records of audit logs to Azure Blob Storage. Furthermore, to facilitate seamless experience, and maintain easy retrieval of evidence from records, we built a custom API and a React web application, which we embedded into the existing CRM application so it is available to lending officers, and they can easily retrieve any historical audits they may wish to search, without interrupting their workflow. The process utilized batch processing, paging and throttling features to quickly extract and move large numbers of audit trail records away from a single institution. Using Archiving allows agri-lending institutions to develop a low-burden, high-performance CRM environment while maintaining historical data to meet credit risk assessment and regulatory requirements. Deliverables include up to 80% reduced storage maintenance costs, more responsiveness from the CRM, regulatory reliability, and improved decision-making by loan officers. Additionally, the approach is validated through a case study using AgriBank, indicating a considerable savings at the operations level and improved loan officer productivity. Future research studies could explore predictive analytics associated with artificial intelligence using archived data, which could assist in increasing agricultural credit decisions.
