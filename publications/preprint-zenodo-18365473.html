AI-Driven Personalized Yoga Instruction: Enhancing User Experience Through Adaptive Feedback Systems

Authors: Laxminarayan S Baviskar; Karthik Nallani Chakravartula; RushilKumar Patel; P.R. Sanjaya; Punam Kumari; Yatu Rani

Status: Accepted / In progress publishing (preprint)

PDF: Download
Zenodo record: https://zenodo.org/records/18365473

Abstract

Systems with artificial intelligence (AI) are able to learn and adjust over time in response to human input and changing demands for stress management. Long-term relevance and efficacy of the customised yoga experience are guaranteed by this adaptive learning process. Numerous facets of how artificial intelligence might improve the efficiency of yoga practices in lowering stress can be explored in research on AI with yoga for stress management. The convolutional Markov graph vector technique is used in this study to personalise the yoga positions. Chronic stress has wide-ranging effects that might affect one's physical and mental well-being. Conditions including anxiety, sadness, heart disease, and weakened immune systems are linked to elevated stress levels. AI-driven exercise solutions may advance in the future with wearable integration, injury avoidance, and personalised yoga regimens.
